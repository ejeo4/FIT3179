Sustainability of Countries Around the World (2014)

Biocapacity and Ecological Footprint are two tools created by the Global Footprint Network, used to study and measure human impact on the environment. The dataset, taken from the Global Footprint Network and used in this visualisation, measures Biocapacity and Ecological Footprint of Consumption in Global Hectares per Capita.

Biocapacity is measured in Global Hectares per Capita (per person) and refers to the capacity of ecosystems' ability to produce biological materials used by people, whilst absorbing waste materials generated by humans.

Ecological Footprint of Consumption is measured in Global Hectares per Capita (per person) and refers to the area used to support a population's consumption, including the area needed to produce the consumed materials as well as the area needed to absorb the carbon dioxide emissions.

Any countries with a higher Ecological Footprint compared to Biocapacity, can be considered unsustainable.

Top Countries Breakdown

The bar charts to the right shows the types of areas that make up the total Biocapacity and total Ecological Footprint.

Any data inputs with value 0 can be assumed that there was no reliable data available.

Highest Population

China was recorded to have the highest population in 2014.

The bar charts indicate that China has a higher Ecological Footprint compared to its Biocapacity, so thus, in 2014, China was considered unsustainable.

Highest Biocapacity

French Guiana was recorded to have the highest Biocapacity per Capita in 2014.

From the charts, it is clear that the large areas of Forest land was the main contribution to the country's high Biocapacity measures.

Highest Ecological Footprint of Consumption

Qatar was recorded to have the highest Ecological Footprint of Consumption per Capita in 2014.

The chart breakdown indicates that the fishing grounds make up most of the country's Biocapacity and that there was no reliable data available to measure the forest land for Biocapacity in 2014.
